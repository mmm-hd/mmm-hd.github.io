# Summer semester 2026

## Mathematical Modelling in Medicine

This course introduces mathematical modelling techniques for medical
applications, including components driven by modern machine learning,
using inverse treatment planning (ITP) in brachytherapy as a running
example. In the first part of the course we develop a model-based
approach: starting from basic principles of radiation transport we
derive a mathematical model for dose deposition, and formulate
treatment planning as an inverse problem and optimization task. We
discuss objective functions, clinical constraints, and numerical
optimization methods for computing treatment plans.

In the second part of the course we introduce basic concepts of
machine learning and neural networks. We discuss convolutional neural
networks and the U-Net architecture for medical image segmentation and
analyze how learning-based methods can be integrated into the ITP
pipeline. Throughout the course we compare model-based and data-driven
approaches and discuss in which parts of the pipeline machine learning
methods are beneficial and where classical mathematical models remain
essential. As an outlook, we discuss recent developments in
learning-based optimization, where machine learning techniques are
used to guide the solution of optimization problems.
